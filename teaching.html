Teaching & Courses

Innovative education that bridges theory and practice in data science and visualization

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on creating an inclusive, hands-on learning environment where students can explore complex concepts through practical application. I believe in fostering critical thinking, encouraging collaboration, and providing real-world contexts that make abstract concepts tangible and meaningful.

Through project-based learning, interactive demonstrations, and mentored research experiences, I guide students to develop both technical proficiency and the analytical mindset necessary for success in their academic and professional careers.

Current Courses

Data Visualization & Interactive Design

CS/STAT 4XX • Fall 2024

An advanced course focusing on the principles and practices of effective data visualization. Students learn to create compelling visual narratives using modern tools like D3.js, Python visualization libraries, and interactive web technologies.

Topics Covered:
  • Visual encoding principles and perceptual psychology
  • Interactive visualization techniques
  • D3.js and web-based visualization development
  • Statistical graphics and exploratory data analysis
  • Dashboard design and user experience

Applied Data Science Methods

CS 3XX • Spring 2024

A practical course that bridges the gap between statistical theory and real-world data science applications. Students work with industry datasets and learn to communicate findings effectively to both technical and non-technical audiences.

Key Projects:
  • Predictive modeling for business applications
  • Time series analysis and forecasting
  • Text mining and sentiment analysis
  • A/B testing and experimental design
  • Capstone project with industry partner

Computational Methods for Research

GRAD 6XX • Fall 2024

A graduate-level seminar designed for researchers across disciplines who want to incorporate computational methods into their work. Emphasis on reproducible research practices and collaborative development workflows.

Learning Outcomes:
  • Version control and collaborative coding with Git
  • Reproducible research with R Markdown and Jupyter
  • High-performance computing and parallel processing
  • API development and data pipeline construction
  • Best practices in research software development
