§ 01 — Philosophy

My mission is to create equitable educational opportunities and nurture potential. Central to my approach is shifting focus from the perceived quality of students to the effectiveness of the learning design — so that every student, regardless of background, can reach their full potential.

I believe in cultivating caring relationships with students and celebrating what makes each one unique. The job isn't just to impart knowledge; it's to discover, nurture, and help each student shine in their own way.

§ 02 — Courses taught

DATA 5630 · developed from scratch · Spring 2023

Deep Forecasting — Advanced Time Series

Classical time series models meet modern machine learning and deep learning. Launched as a special topic; its success made it a permanent part of the curriculum.

Key areas

  • Time series fundamentals and stationarity
  • Exponential smoothing and ARIMA/SARIMA
  • Machine learning for time series
  • Deep sequence models (RNN/LSTM)
  • Prophet, NeuralProphet, and AutoML tools
  • Production-ready deployment

DATA 5600 · DAIS department · ongoing

Machine Learning

The foundational course of the Data Analytics track. Real-time coding sessions on Google Colab so concepts get applied — and tested — immediately.

Key areas

  • Supervised and unsupervised learning
  • Model evaluation and validation
  • Feature engineering and selection
  • Practical implementation, end to end

DATA 5610 · DAIS department · ongoing

Deep Learning

From fundamentals to the frontier: modern architectures for vision and sequence modeling, taught with working code.

Key areas

  • Deep neural networks (NN, DNN)
  • Convolutional networks (CNN, R-CNN, YOLO, FCN)
  • Recurrent networks (RNN, LSTM)
  • Transformers and attention

USU 1010 · first-year experience · Fall 2023

University Connections

Helping freshmen find their footing in college. Teaching this course keeps me close to the challenges new students actually face — and it shapes how I teach everything else.
