Teaching & Courses

Innovative education that bridges theory and practice in data science and visualization

Teaching Philosophy

My mission as an educator is to create equitable educational opportunities and nurture potential. Central to my approach is shifting focus from the perceived quality of students to the effectiveness of the learning design, ensuring that all students, regardless of their background, can achieve their full potential.

I believe in cultivating caring relationships with students and celebrating the uniqueness of each individual. My role has evolved to not only impart knowledge but also to discover, nurture, and help each student shine in their own way through inclusive learning environments and personalized education.

Courses Taught

Since 2018, I have taught over 8 different courses covering more than 30 sections, teaching over 600 students. I have developed 2 courses from scratch and offered them as new additions to our curriculum.

DATA 5630: Deep Forecasting

Developed from scratch • Spring 2023

A unique course combining advanced time series forecasting techniques with deep learning approaches, bridging the gap between traditional statistical methods and cutting-edge AI applications. Initially launched as a special topic, its success led to permanent inclusion in our curriculum.

Topics Covered:
  • Advanced time series forecasting techniques
  • Deep learning approaches to forecasting
  • Neural networks for temporal data
  • Real-world forecasting applications
  • Model evaluation and validation

ECN 5090: Machine Learning in Economics and Finance

Developed from scratch • Economics & Finance Dept

Recognizing the growing importance of machine learning in economic and financial analysis, I developed this course to integrate machine learning principles within economic and financial contexts, equipping students with skills to apply these powerful tools to real-world financial problems.

Key Areas:
  • Machine learning in financial modeling
  • Algorithmic trading strategies
  • Risk assessment and portfolio optimization
  • Economic forecasting with ML
  • Financial data analysis and interpretation

DATA 5600/5610: Machine Learning & Advanced Machine Learning

DAIS Department • Ongoing

Core courses in the Data Analytics track covering fundamental and advanced machine learning concepts. These courses emphasize hands-on learning with real-time coding sessions using platforms like Google Colab, allowing students to immediately apply concepts and receive instant feedback.

Teaching Innovations:
  • Interactive real-time coding sessions
  • Industry-aligned project-based learning
  • Integration with Kaggle competitions
  • AI-augmented learning tools
  • Sentiment analysis feedback system

USU 1010: University Connections

First-year Experience • Fall 2023

A course designed to help freshmen transition to college life. This experience provides valuable insights into challenges faced by new students and informs my overall teaching approach across all courses.
