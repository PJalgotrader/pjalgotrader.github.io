Research & Mentorship

Collaborative research advancing the frontiers of data science and educational technology

Research Interests

Visual Analytics & Perception

Investigating how humans process and interpret visual information in data visualizations. Research focuses on cognitive load, attention patterns, and design principles that enhance comprehension and decision-making.

Educational Data Science

Developing innovative pedagogical approaches for teaching data science and computational thinking. Studies include learning analytics, adaptive learning systems, and the effectiveness of interactive learning environments.

Social Computing

Analyzing social networks, online communities, and digital behavior patterns. Research explores information diffusion, community detection, and the impact of social media on public opinion and decision-making processes.

Current Research Projects

Cognitive Load in Interactive Visualizations

Funding: NSF Grant #12345 • Duration: 2023-2026

Collaborators: Dr. Sarah Chen (Psychology), Dr. Michael Rodriguez (HCI)

This interdisciplinary project investigates how different visualization designs affect cognitive load and user performance. Using eye-tracking technology and cognitive assessment tools, we're developing guidelines for creating more effective and accessible data visualizations.

Recent Publications:
  • "Eye-tracking Analysis of Dashboard Design Patterns" - IEEE VIS 2024
  • "Cognitive Load Metrics for Visualization Evaluation" - CHI 2024

Adaptive Learning Systems for Data Science Education

Funding: Utah State University URCO Grant • Duration: 2024-2025

Student Researchers: 3 Graduate Students, 5 Undergraduate Students

Developing an AI-powered learning platform that adapts to individual student needs in data science courses. The system uses learning analytics to personalize content delivery and provide targeted feedback based on student performance patterns.

Key Innovations:
  • Real-time difficulty adjustment based on student progress
  • Personalized code review and feedback system
  • Collaborative problem-solving environments

Social Media Impact on Democratic Processes

Collaboration: Political Science Department • Duration: 2022-2024

Data: 10M+ tweets, survey responses from 5,000 participants

A comprehensive analysis of how social media platforms influence political discourse and voter behavior. This project combines network analysis, sentiment analysis, and survey research to understand information propagation in digital political spaces.

Findings:
  • Echo chambers are less prevalent than commonly assumed
  • Cross-cutting exposure varies significantly by platform
  • Visual content has 3x higher engagement than text-only posts
